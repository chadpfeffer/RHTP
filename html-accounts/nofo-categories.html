NOFO Category Reference (A–K)

Every allowable use of funds from the CMS-RHT-26-001 NOFO, with verbatim CMS text, any applicable caps, and Cisco positioning for each.
THE #1 RULE of RHTP selling: Position Cisco into the clinical outcome categories (G, E, H, A, D, K) wherever possible. These categories are scored on measurable clinical outcomes (time-to-specialist, workforce retention, behavioral health access, chronic disease improvement), which is where state evaluators are rewarded. Category F (IT advances) is a legitimate home for security specifically — CMS names "cybersecurity capability development" as an allowable F use — but everything else positioned as IT loses the outcome-scoring leverage.

Quick primer: Under Public Law 119-21, Congress established the $50B RHT Program to distribute $10B/year from FY2026–2030 across all 50 states. Each state writes its own application and must use its awarded funds for at least three of the eleven permissible uses listed below (Section 71401 of P.L. 119-21). Categories B and J are capped (15% and 20% respectively); the rest are uncapped but subject to restrictions in the NOFO's funding policies and limitations section.

A — Prevention and chronic disease

No cap
CMS Official NOFO text — verbatim
Promoting evidence-based, measurable interventions to improve prevention and chronic disease management.
— CMS-RHT-26-001, Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO), §"Use of funds", pp. 11–12
Plain English: Money for programs that prevent disease or help people manage chronic conditions (diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, cancer screening). The state has to show measurable outcomes — not just "we built a thing," but "we reduced HbA1c by X% in Y population."

B — Provider payments

15% CAP
CMS Official NOFO text — verbatim
Providing payments to health care providers for the provision of health care items or services, subject to restrictions described in the funding policies and limitations.
— CMS-RHT-26-001, Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO), §"Use of funds", pp. 11–12
CMS cap / restriction — verbatim
Funding for provider payments, as described in category B of the program requirements and expectations use of funds section, cannot exceed 15% of the total funding CMS awards States in a given budget period.
— CMS-RHT-26-001, NOFO, §"Program-specific limitations / Unallowable costs", pp. 19–20
Plain English: Direct reimbursement to providers for care delivery. Capped at 15% of the state's total RHTP award and subject to strict rules: the NOFO will not allow payments that duplicate billable services or alter existing fee schedules. This is not a natural fit for Cisco — it's provider reimbursement, not infrastructure.

E — Workforce

No cap
CMS Official NOFO text — verbatim
Recruiting and retaining clinical workforce talent to rural areas, with commitments to serve rural communities for a minimum of 5 years.
— CMS-RHT-26-001, Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO), §"Use of funds", pp. 11–12

Note the 5-year service commitment is a CMS requirement.

F — IT advances

No blanket cap (see EMR restriction)
CMS Official NOFO text — verbatim
Providing technical assistance, software, and hardware for significant information technology advances designed to improve efficiency, enhance cybersecurity capability development, and improve patient health outcomes.
— CMS-RHT-26-001, Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO), §"Use of funds", pp. 11–12
CMS cap / restriction — verbatim
No more than 5% of total funding CMS awards to a State in a given budget period can support funding the replacement of an EMR system if a previous HITECH certified EMR system is already in place as of September 1, 2025.
— CMS-RHT-26-001, NOFO, §"Program-specific limitations / Unallowable costs", pp. 19–20
Plain English: IMPORTANT CORRECTION: F is NOT capped at 5% overall. The 5% cap applies ONLY to EMR replacement in states that already have a HITECH-certified EMR. F itself has no blanket cap. However, F is still where you DON'T want to position Cisco — not because of a cap, but because F is scored on efficiency and cybersecurity improvements, while G/E/H/A are scored on clinical outcomes. The clinical categories give you outcome metrics the state RFP evaluators reward heavily. Same products, better scoring posture.

G — Appropriate care availability

No cap
CMS Official NOFO text — verbatim
Assisting rural communities to right size their health care delivery systems by identifying needed preventative, ambulatory, pre-hospital, emergency, acute inpatient care, outpatient care, and post-acute care service lines.
— CMS-RHT-26-001, Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO), §"Use of funds", pp. 11–12
Plain English: The most natural home for Cisco. "Right-size care delivery" across the full continuum (preventative through post-acute) means connecting rural patients to the care they need — and everything Cisco builds in telehealth, care coordination, and clinical communications lives here. G is uncapped and is a scored category.

J — Capital expenditures and infrastructure

20% CAP
CMS Official NOFO text — verbatim
Investing in existing rural health care facility buildings and infrastructure, including minor building alterations or renovations and equipment upgrades to ensure long-term overhead and upkeep costs are commensurate with patient volume, subject to restrictions in the funding policies and limitations.
— CMS-RHT-26-001, Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO), §"Use of funds", pp. 11–12
CMS cap / restriction — verbatim
Category J funding cannot exceed 20% of the total funding CMS awards States in a given budget period. [Separately: "New construction is unallowable. Supplanting funding for in-process or planned construction projects or directing funding towards new construction builds is unallowable."]
— CMS-RHT-26-001, NOFO, §"Program-specific limitations / Unallowable costs", pp. 19–20
Plain English: Any hardware purchase or facility upgrade counts against J's 20% cap. New construction is flatly prohibited. The good news: Cisco Capital offers an operating-lease structure (under 2 CFR 200.465) that treats Cisco hardware as rental cost — i.e., operating expense — which keeps the grant under J's 20% cap. This is the "CapEx to OpEx" conversion every CFO should hear about.

Caps and restrictions — quick reference

WhereRestriction (verbatim where possible)
Category BProvider payments cannot exceed 15% of total state award per budget period.
Category JCapital expenditures cannot exceed 20% of total state award per budget period. New construction is unallowable.
Category F (narrow)5% cap on EMR replacement, and only if a HITECH-certified EMR was already in place as of September 1, 2025. This is NOT a cap on all of F.
Administrative expensesNo more than 10% of funds may be used for administrative expenses.
Rural Tech Catalyst Fund-style initiativesCannot exceed the lesser of 10% of state award or $20M per budget period.
